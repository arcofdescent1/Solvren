## Overview

Revenue Impact Reports analyze the potential financial consequences of a change.

These reports help reviewers understand:

• potential failure modes  
• financial exposure  
• recommended safeguards

The report is generated by the AI Risk Engine using structured change data.

---

## Why Revenue Impact Analysis Matters

Revenue operations systems control critical financial processes.

Even small configuration errors can lead to:

• incorrect pricing  
• billing failures  
• duplicate charges  
• inaccurate financial reporting

Revenue Impact Reports help teams evaluate these risks before deployment.

### Risk Score

The risk score summarizes the potential financial impact of a change.

Factors influencing risk include:

• domain  
• systems involved  
• deployment scope  
• operational complexity

Higher scores indicate greater potential risk.

---

### Failure Modes

Failure modes describe ways the change could produce unintended outcomes.

Examples include:

• incorrect pricing rules  
• billing duplication  
• invoice generation failures  
• reporting discrepancies

Understanding these scenarios helps reviewers evaluate risk.

---

### Safeguard Recommendations

The system may recommend safeguards such as:

• additional approvals  
• expanded monitoring  
• additional validation testing

These safeguards help mitigate identified risks.

---

### Executive Summary

The executive summary provides a concise explanation of the change and its risk profile.

This section helps stakeholders quickly understand the implications of the change.

---

## How Reviewers Use the Report

Reviewers evaluate the report when deciding whether to approve a change.

They consider:

• the severity of potential failure modes  
• whether safeguards are sufficient  
• whether additional validation is needed

This structured evaluation improves governance consistency.

---

## Key Principle

Revenue Impact Reports help organizations answer an essential question:

**"What financial risks could this change introduce?"**
